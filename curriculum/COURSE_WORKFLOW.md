# Health Design Sprint: Complete Course Workflow

## Overview

This document outlines the 14-phase workflow students follow during the 2-week Health Design Sprint elective. Each phase builds on the previous, guiding teams from problem discovery through handoff of a functional prototype.

**Total Course Duration**: 10 days (2 weeks, weekdays only)

---

## Week 1: Discover & Define

---

### Phase 1: Problem Exploration

**Core Question**: *"What's actually going on?"*

**Duration**: 4 hours

#### Learning Outcomes

| Knowledge | Skills | Mindset |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| Understand systems thinking in healthcare contexts | Observe clinical environments without judgment | Embrace beginner's mind—suspend assumptions |
| Recognize difference between symptoms and root causes | Document observations systematically | Cultivate curiosity over certainty |
| Know common categories of healthcare friction points | Ask open-ended exploratory questions | Accept ambiguity as part of discovery |

#### Recommended AI Tools

- **Claude/ChatGPT**: Background research on clinical domains, understanding medical terminology
- **Perplexity**: Quick literature scans on known problems in the focus area
- **Otter.ai**: Transcribe initial conversations and observations

#### Key Deliverables

- [ ] Problem domain map (visual representation of the space)
- [ ] Initial observations log (minimum 20 observations)
- [ ] List of 5-10 potential problem areas to investigate
- [ ] Assumptions inventory (what do we think we know?)

---

### Phase 2: User Identification

**Core Question**: *"Who's affected?"*

**Duration**: 2 hours

#### Learning Outcomes

| Knowledge | Skills | Mindset |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| Understand stakeholder mapping frameworks | Create comprehensive stakeholder maps | Value all perspectives, not just clinical ones |
| Recognize primary, secondary, and tertiary users | Identify power dynamics and relationships | See patients as experts in their experience |
| Know the difference between users, customers, and beneficiaries | Prioritize stakeholders for research | Recognize that "users" includes families and caregivers |

#### Recommended AI Tools

- **Claude/ChatGPT**: Generate comprehensive stakeholder lists for healthcare scenarios
- **Miro AI**: Auto-organize stakeholder maps and relationship diagrams
- **Whimsical AI**: Create visual stakeholder ecosystem maps

#### Key Deliverables

- [ ] Stakeholder map with all affected parties identified
- [ ] Stakeholder prioritization matrix (influence vs. impact)
- [ ] Primary user group(s) selected for deep research
- [ ] Recruitment plan for user research participants

---

### Phase 3: User Research

**Core Question**: *"What's life like for them?"*

**Duration**: 8 hours

#### Learning Outcomes

| Knowledge | Skills | Mindset |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| Understand ethnographic research methods | Conduct empathetic interviews (45-60 min) | Listen to understand, not to respond |
| Know interview best practices and question types | Perform contextual observation | Treat interviewees as teachers |
| Recognize cognitive biases in research | Take effective field notes | Stay present—avoid jumping to solutions |
| Understand informed consent in design research | Capture quotes, behaviors, and environment | Honor vulnerability with confidentiality |

#### Recommended AI Tools

- **Otter.ai/Rev**: Transcribe interviews with speaker identification
- **Dovetail**: Organize and tag qualitative research data
- **Claude**: Generate interview guides, suggest follow-up questions
- **Zoom/Teams AI**: Auto-summarize recorded sessions

#### Key Deliverables

- [ ] Completed interview guide with 15-20 questions
- [ ] Minimum 4-6 user interviews conducted (per team)
- [ ] 2+ hours of contextual observation documented
- [ ] Raw research data organized and accessible
- [ ] Initial emotional journey captured for each participant

---

### Phase 4: Insight Synthesis

**Core Question**: *"What patterns emerge?"*

**Duration**: 4 hours

#### Learning Outcomes

| Knowledge | Skills | Mindset |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| Understand affinity diagramming and clustering | Synthesize qualitative data into themes | Trust the process—patterns will emerge |
| Know the structure of insight statements | Create compelling user personas | Let data lead, not preconceptions |
| Recognize strong vs. weak insights | Build journey maps from research | Value tension and contradiction as signals |
| Understand jobs-to-be-done framework | Write insight statements that reveal tension | Synthesis is creative, not mechanical |

#### Recommended AI Tools

- **Claude/ChatGPT**: Analyze interview transcripts for themes, generate initial persona drafts
- **Miro AI**: Cluster sticky notes, suggest groupings
- **Notion AI**: Summarize research documents, extract key quotes
- **Dovetail**: Tag and analyze qualitative data at scale

#### Key Deliverables

- [ ] Affinity diagram with clustered observations
- [ ] 2-3 user personas based on research patterns
- [ ] User journey map showing current experience
- [ ] 3-5 insight statements (format: "We learned that [user] needs [need] because [insight]")
- [ ] Key quotes board highlighting user voice

---

### Phase 5: Design Brief

**Core Question**: *"What problem are we solving?"*

**Duration**: 2 hours

#### Learning Outcomes

| Knowledge | Skills | Mindset |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| Understand problem framing and reframing | Write clear "How Might We" questions | Narrow focus to expand possibilities |
| Know criteria for well-scoped design challenges | Scope problems to actionable size | A good problem statement is half the solution |
| Recognize the difference between problem and solution space | Align team on shared problem definition | Commit to a direction while staying curious |
| Understand design constraints and their value | Identify constraints and freedoms | Constraints enable creativity |

#### Recommended AI Tools

- **Claude/ChatGPT**: Reframe problems, generate alternative HMW phrasings
- **Miro AI**: Facilitate problem prioritization exercises
- **Notion AI**: Document and refine design brief

#### Key Deliverables

- [ ] Primary "How Might We" question (team aligned)
- [ ] 2-3 secondary HMW questions
- [ ] Design brief document including:
  - Target user and context
  - Core insight being addressed
  - Success criteria
  - Known constraints
  - Out of scope items
- [ ] Team alignment check (all members can articulate the problem)

---

### Phase 6: Ideation

**Core Question**: *"What might we build?"*

**Duration**: 4 hours

#### Learning Outcomes

| Knowledge | Skills | Mindset |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| Understand divergent thinking techniques | Facilitate brainstorming sessions | Quantity breeds quality—defer judgment |
| Know multiple ideation methods (SCAMPER, Crazy 8s, etc.) | Sketch ideas quickly and visually | Build on others' ideas generously |
| Recognize premature convergence | Generate 50+ ideas per session | Wild ideas unlock adjacent possibilities |
| Understand the value of analogous inspiration | Find inspiration from other industries | Every idea has a kernel of value |

#### Recommended AI Tools

- **Claude/ChatGPT**: Generate diverse solution concepts, provide analogous examples from other fields
- **Midjourney/DALL-E**: Visualize abstract concepts quickly
- **Miro AI**: Facilitate digital brainstorming, suggest idea combinations
- **Whimsical AI**: Quick concept sketching and diagramming

#### Key Deliverables

- [ ] Minimum 50 ideas generated (documented)
- [ ] Ideas organized by theme or approach
- [ ] Crazy 8s sketches from each team member
- [ ] 10-15 ideas selected for deeper exploration
- [ ] Inspiration board with analogous solutions

---

### Phase 7: Concept Selection

**Core Question**: *"Which idea tests our insight?"*

**Duration**: 2 hours

#### Learning Outcomes

| Knowledge | Skills | Mindset |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| Understand concept evaluation frameworks | Apply selection criteria objectively | Choose based on learning potential, not certainty |
| Know feasibility vs. desirability vs. viability | Facilitate democratic decision-making | The "best" idea is the one you can test fastest |
| Recognize selection bias and groupthink | Combine and evolve concepts | Kill your darlings—don't marry ideas |
| Understand assumption mapping | Identify riskiest assumptions to test | Selection is a hypothesis, not a verdict |

#### Recommended AI Tools

- **Claude/ChatGPT**: Evaluate concepts against criteria, identify blind spots
- **Miro AI**: Facilitate dot voting and prioritization matrices
- **Notion AI**: Document selection rationale

#### Key Deliverables

- [ ] Evaluation matrix with weighted criteria
- [ ] Top 3 concepts with documented rationale
- [ ] Primary concept selected for prototyping
- [ ] Assumption map for selected concept
- [ ] Riskiest assumption identified for testing
- [ ] Pivot options identified (backup directions)

---

## Week 2: Develop & Deliver

---

### Phase 8: Low-Fi Prototyping

**Core Question**: *"Can we show the concept?"*

**Duration**: 4 hours

#### Learning Outcomes

| Knowledge | Skills | Mindset |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| Understand prototype fidelity spectrum | Create paper prototypes rapidly | Done is better than perfect |
| Know when to use different prototype types | Build clickable wireframes | Prototypes are questions, not answers |
| Recognize the purpose of low-fi (test concepts, not polish) | Storyboard user scenarios | If you're not embarrassed, you waited too long |
| Understand the "Wizard of Oz" technique | Simulate functionality without building | Fake it strategically to learn faster |

#### Recommended AI Tools

- **Claude/ChatGPT**: Generate UI copy, user flows, edge case scenarios
- **Figma AI**: Auto-generate wireframe layouts
- **v0 by Vercel**: Generate UI components from descriptions
- **Midjourney/DALL-E**: Create placeholder visuals and icons
- **Miro AI**: Create quick user flow diagrams

#### Key Deliverables

- [ ] Paper prototype or low-fi digital mockup
- [ ] Key screens/interactions mapped (5-10 screens)
- [ ] User testing script prepared
- [ ] Storyboard showing concept in use context
- [ ] List of questions the prototype should answer

---

### Phase 9: User Feedback Round 1

**Core Question**: *"Does this resonate?"*

**Duration**: 4 hours

#### Learning Outcomes

| Knowledge | Skills | Mindset |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| Understand usability testing methods | Facilitate prototype walkthroughs | Feedback is a gift—receive it openly |
| Know how to write neutral test scripts | Observe without leading | You are not your prototype |
| Recognize confirmation bias in testing | Document feedback systematically | Negative feedback is the most valuable |
| Understand think-aloud protocol | Distinguish opinions from behaviors | Test the concept, not the aesthetics |

#### Recommended AI Tools

- **Otter.ai**: Transcribe feedback sessions
- **Loom**: Record screen + user reactions
- **Claude/ChatGPT**: Analyze feedback for patterns, suggest iterations
- **Notion AI**: Organize and synthesize feedback

#### Key Deliverables

- [ ] 3-5 user testing sessions completed
- [ ] Feedback organized by theme
- [ ] Critical issues identified and prioritized
- [ ] Validated assumptions documented
- [ ] Invalidated assumptions documented
- [ ] Iteration priorities for high-fi prototype

---

### Phase 10: High-Fi Prototyping

**Core Question**: *"Can we build something functional?"*

**Duration**: 8 hours

#### Learning Outcomes

| Knowledge | Skills | Mindset |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| Understand functional prototype requirements | Use AI coding assistants effectively | AI amplifies—you still drive |
| Know no-code/low-code platform options | Integrate APIs and data sources | Focus on core functionality first |
| Recognize technical feasibility constraints | Debug with AI assistance | "Good enough" enables learning |
| Understand MVP (Minimum Viable Prototype) thinking | Create realistic user interactions | Ship to learn, not to impress |

#### Recommended AI Tools

- **Claude/Cursor**: Write and debug code, explain technical concepts
- **v0 by Vercel**: Generate React components from descriptions
- **Replit AI**: Full-stack development with AI assistance
- **Bolt.new**: Rapid full-stack prototyping
- **Lovable/GPT Engineer**: Generate functional apps from descriptions
- **Figma + plugins**: High-fidelity design with export to code

#### Key Deliverables

- [ ] Functional prototype (deployable/shareable link)
- [ ] Core user flow working end-to-end
- [ ] Realistic sample data populated
- [ ] Known limitations documented
- [ ] Testing script updated for functional prototype
- [ ] Technical architecture documented (simple diagram)

---

### Phase 11: User Feedback Round 2

**Core Question**: *"Does this work?"*

**Duration**: 4 hours

#### Learning Outcomes

| Knowledge | Skills | Mindset |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| Understand functional testing vs. concept testing | Conduct task-based usability testing | Real behavior > stated preferences |
| Know how to measure task success | Capture quantitative + qualitative data | Observe the struggle—that's the signal |
| Recognize when to pivot vs. iterate | Identify usability vs. desirability issues | Users blame themselves; designers look deeper |
| Understand the difference between bugs and design flaws | Prioritize fixes by impact | Almost done is the most dangerous phase |

#### Recommended AI Tools

- **Loom**: Record user testing sessions
- **Hotjar/FullStory**: Track user behavior in prototype
- **Claude/ChatGPT**: Analyze feedback, suggest fixes
- **Otter.ai**: Transcribe verbal feedback

#### Key Deliverables

- [ ] 4-6 user testing sessions with functional prototype
- [ ] Task success rate documented
- [ ] Critical usability issues identified
- [ ] Desirability feedback captured
- [ ] Final iteration priorities set
- [ ] Go/no-go decision for current direction

---

### Phase 12: Demo to Reliable System

**Core Question**: *"How do we make it reproducible?"*

**Duration**: 4 hours

#### Learning Outcomes

| Knowledge | Skills | Mindset |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| Understand system documentation requirements | Write clear setup instructions | If it's not documented, it doesn't exist |
| Know basics of deployment and hosting | Create user guides and training materials | Design for the next team, not just users |
| Recognize sustainability considerations | Record demo walkthroughs | Handoff quality = project impact |
| Understand technical debt and maintenance | Document known issues and workarounds | Polish matters for credibility |

#### Recommended AI Tools

- **Claude/ChatGPT**: Generate documentation, README files, user guides
- **Loom**: Record video walkthroughs and tutorials
- **Scribe**: Auto-generate step-by-step guides
- **Notion AI**: Organize and format documentation

#### Key Deliverables

- [ ] Polished, stable prototype
- [ ] Setup/installation guide (if applicable)
- [ ] User guide with screenshots
- [ ] Video walkthrough of key features
- [ ] Known issues and future recommendations
- [ ] Source code/project files organized

---

### Phase 13: Final Presentation

**Core Question**: *"What did we learn?"*

**Duration**: 4 hours (2 hours prep, 2 hours presentations)

#### Learning Outcomes

| Knowledge | Skills | Mindset |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| Understand storytelling frameworks for design | Create compelling presentation narrative | The journey matters as much as the outcome |
| Know how to present to diverse stakeholders | Deliver clear, time-boxed presentations | Own failures as learning opportunities |
| Recognize the importance of the "story arc" | Handle Q&A with confidence | Present with humility and conviction |
| Understand how to frame impact and learning | Demo live without disaster | Celebrate the process, not just results |

#### Recommended AI Tools

- **Claude/ChatGPT**: Outline presentation, refine narrative
- **Gamma**: Generate presentation slides from outline
- **Beautiful.ai**: Auto-design presentation slides
- **Loom**: Record backup demo video

#### Key Deliverables

- [ ] 10-minute presentation deck
- [ ] Live demo prepared (with backup video)
- [ ] Presentation covers:
  - Problem and user insight
  - Solution and key features
  - Testing results and iterations
  - Learnings and recommendations
- [ ] Q&A preparation notes
- [ ] Presentation rehearsed (minimum 2x)

---

### Phase 14: Handoff Package

**Core Question**: *"How does this continue?"*

**Duration**: 2 hours

#### Learning Outcomes

| Knowledge | Skills | Mindset |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| Understand handoff requirements for continuity | Compile comprehensive handoff documentation | Your work's legacy is in the handoff |
| Know intellectual property considerations in healthcare | Organize assets for transfer | Enable others to build on your foundation |
| Recognize stakeholders who might continue the work | Write actionable recommendations | Endings are beginnings for others |
| Understand pathways from prototype to implementation | Facilitate knowledge transfer sessions | Leave it better than you found it |

#### Recommended AI Tools

- **Claude/ChatGPT**: Generate handoff documentation templates, summarize project
- **Notion AI**: Organize final project repository
- **Loom**: Record knowledge transfer videos
- **Scribe**: Document key processes step-by-step

#### Key Deliverables

- [ ] Complete project repository with all assets
- [ ] Executive summary (1-2 pages)
- [ ] Research archive (all raw data, organized)
- [ ] Design assets (all files, organized)
- [ ] Technical documentation
- [ ] Roadmap for future development
- [ ] Contact information for team members
- [ ] Handoff meeting completed with stakeholders

## Notes for Facilitators

- Phase durations are estimates; adjust based on team progress

- The goal is learning through doing, not perfection
- AI tools are accelerators, not replacements for critical thinking
